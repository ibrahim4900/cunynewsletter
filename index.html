CUNY has forgiven $95 million in unpaid debt for more than 50,000 students, and there are another 18,000 students still potentially eligible to have their debt erased. You have one month left to fill out the simple CUNY Comeback application in CUNYfirst, so do it soon! You have nothing to lose but your debt!

We’re reimagining grad school at CUNY! As the higher education landscape continues to evolve, a panel of students, faculty and administrators is embarking on a two-year mission to make CUNY a world-class model for graduate education and research.

CUNY will help prepare a new generation of Community Health Workers through the city’s Public Health Corps with a focus on health equity across the five boroughs.

NYC will be ready to face the next pandemic thanks to the city’s first ever NYC Pandemic Response Institute, with one CUNY at the forefront.

STUDENT CENTER
Deborah Leter, a third-year Ph.D. student at the Graduate Center, is studying cultural anthropology while teaching at Hunter and researching France-Africa relations and colonial legacies. She also serves as communications co-chair for the Doctoral and Graduate Students’ Council, the GC student government, in her second year on the DGSC.
 
What drives Deborah? She has a vested interest in interdisciplinary research and documentary filmmaking. Her experience as a Franco-American who was raised in France informs her research focus and Francophone postcolonial theory.
